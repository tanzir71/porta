Install Porta 1.0

Up and sharing in a minute.

The current release is ad-hoc signed, so macOS needs one manual approval.

1

Download the DMG

Use the official GitHub Release link on this page.

2

Move Porta to Applications

Open the disk image, then drag Porta into the Applications folder.

3

Try opening Porta once

macOS will likely say it cannot verify the developer. Dismiss that message.

4

Choose Open Anyway

Open System Settings → Privacy & Security, scroll to Security, click Open Anyway, then confirm with your password.

Honest security

Public by design.
Private when you add a password.

Porta links are hard to guess, but anyone with the URL can reach the share while it is on. Use password protection for sensitive folders, disable uploads unless needed, and stop the share when you are done.

Good to know

Before you open the door.

Does Porta upload my files somewhere?

No Porta storage service exists. While a share is live, files travel from your Mac through Cloudflare’s tunnel to people using the link. Stop the share and that temporary URL stops serving your folder.

Why does macOS show a security warning?

Version 1.0 is ad-hoc signed rather than Developer ID signed and notarized. macOS therefore cannot verify the developer or confirm that Apple scanned the app. Follow the Open Anyway steps above only for a DMG downloaded from this official repository.

Does closing the window stop my links?

No. Porta keeps running quietly in the menu bar. Turn individual shares off there, or choose Quit Porta to stop the app and all active tunnels.

Can I share a local development server?

Yes. Add a port share—for example localhost:3000—and Porta tunnels it directly. Cloudflare Quick Tunnels do not support Server-Sent Events and are intended for testing, not production hosting.
